Coworking Space vs Café: Which Is Better for Focus in Kigali?

Home › Insights › Coworking Space vs Café in Kigali
June 10, 2026

If you work remotely in Kigali, you have probably debated whether to set up at a cafe or book a desk at a coworking space. Both options have their merits, and the right choice depends on what kind of work you are doing, how long you need to work, and what level of focus you require.

Internet Reliability

Internet is the single most important factor for remote work. Coworking spaces in Kigali like Madar Hub invest in dedicated connections with backup options, so your video calls and file uploads stay stable throughout the day. Cafes typically share a single connection among all customers, and speeds can drop dramatically when the place fills up.

If your work involves video calls, large file transfers, or real-time collaboration, a coworking space is the safer bet. For light tasks like checking email or reading documents, cafe Wi-Fi may be enough.

Noise and Distractions

Cafes have music, conversations, espresso machines, and foot traffic. Some people find this ambient noise helpful for creative work, but it becomes a problem when you need to concentrate or take a call. Coworking spaces are designed for focus. At Madar Hub, the environment is professional and quiet, which makes it easier to get into a productive flow and stay there.

Seating and Comfort

Cafes offer whatever seating they have, which may not be designed for long hours of laptop work. Tables can be too low or too small, chairs lack proper back support, and power outlets are often limited. Coworking spaces provide ergonomic workstations with proper desk height, comfortable chairs, and accessible power outlets at every seat.

Cost Comparison

A cafe with internet might seem cheaper at first. You buy a coffee or a meal and sit for a few hours. But if you stay for a full day, those costs add up, and you may feel pressure to keep ordering. At Madar Hub, a day pass costs 10,000 RWF and includes internet, a dedicated workstation, and coffee on request, with no hidden costs or pressure to buy more.

For regular remote workers, the Fixed Desk Monthly Subscription at 100,000 RWF works out to roughly 3,300 RWF per day over a month, which is often less than what you would spend at a cafe.

Professionalism and Credibility

If you need to meet clients, host a video interview, or build a professional routine, a coworking space sends a stronger signal. You have a consistent address, a clean background for calls, and access to meeting rooms. Cafes are informal by nature, and while they can work for casual meetings, they are not ideal for professional presentations or client-facing work.

The Bottom Line

Cafes are great for a change of pace, a short work session, or casual creative work. But if you need consistent internet, a quiet environment, ergonomic seating, and a professional atmosphere, a coworking space like Madar Hub is the better choice for focused, productive work in Kigali.
