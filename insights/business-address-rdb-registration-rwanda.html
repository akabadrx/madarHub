How to Choose a Business Address for RDB Registration in Rwanda

Home › Insights › Business Address for RDB Registration
June 5, 2026

When you register a business in Rwanda, the Rwanda Development Board (RDB) requires you to provide a physical office address. This is not just a formality. The address you choose appears on your official documents, influences how clients and partners perceive your business, and plays a role in compliance with RDB and the Rwanda Revenue Authority (RRA). Choosing the right address matters from the very beginning.

What RDB Requires for a Business Address

RDB requires a verifiable physical address, not a P.O. Box. This means you need a location where your business can be identified and where official correspondence can be delivered. The address must be supported by documentation, typically including an address authorization letter from the premises owner confirming that your business is registered at that location.

For sole proprietors, small companies, and startups that do not yet have a permanent office, this requirement can be a challenge. Renting a full office space just to satisfy the registration requirement is expensive and often unnecessary at an early stage.

What to Look for in a Business Address Service

When choosing an address service for RDB registration, consider these factors:

  • Physical location: The address must be a real, verifiable premises. RDB may verify the address during or after registration.
  • Address authorization: The service should provide a proper authorization letter or agreement that you can present to RDB.
  • Mail handling: You need a reliable way to receive official documents, tax notices, and business correspondence. A good address service will notify you when mail arrives and hold it securely.
  • Professional setting: A business address in a professional building adds credibility compared to a residential address or a location that looks informal.
  • Continuity: Make sure the address service is stable. If you cancel and the address is no longer valid, you must update your registration with RDB, RRA, banks, and other institutions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake is using a residential address without proper documentation. RDB may ask for proof that the address is a legitimate business location. Another mistake is choosing an address service that does not provide proper authorization documentation, which can delay or complicate your registration.

Some business owners also forget to consider what happens after registration. Your address appears on official documents, tax records, and bank applications. Changing it later means updating every institution where it is registered, which takes time and effort.
