Best Places to Work Remotely in Kigali

Home › Insights › Best Places to Work Remotely in Kigali
June 15, 2026

Kigali has become one of Africa's most attractive cities for remote workers. With its clean streets, growing tech scene, and welcoming atmosphere, it is no surprise that more professionals are choosing to work from Rwanda's capital. But where should you actually sit down and get things done?

Coworking Spaces

Coworking spaces are the top choice for remote workers who need reliable internet, a quiet environment, and a professional setting. In Kigali, Madar Hub's coworking space in Kimironko offers everything a remote worker needs: fast internet, comfortable desks, complimentary coffee, and a community of focused professionals.

A dedicated coworking space gives you consistency. You show up, you sit down, and you work. No hunting for a seat, no worrying about whether the Wi-Fi will hold up during a video call, and no pressure to keep ordering food or drinks. Madar Hub's day passes start at 10,000 RWF, and monthly subscriptions with a fixed desk are available for 100,000 RWF.

Cafes with Internet

Kigali has plenty of cafes that offer Wi-Fi, and some are genuinely pleasant places to work for a few hours. Cafes with internet can be a good option if you need a change of scenery or just want to answer emails over coffee. Areas like Kimironko, Nyarutarama, and Kiyovu have several cafes with decent connections.

However, there are trade-offs. Internet at cafes is often shared among many users and can slow down during peak hours. Noise levels fluctuate, seating is not always ergonomic, and you may feel obligated to keep ordering. For part-time or casual work, cafes work fine. For serious focus, a coworking space is the better choice.

Libraries and Study Spaces

The Kigali Public Library and similar community spaces offer quiet environments for focused work. These spaces are free or very affordable, but they come with limitations. Internet access may be slow or unavailable, power outlets can be scarce, and hours are often restricted to weekdays and daytime.

Madar Hub's student day pass at 3,000 RWF offers a middle ground for those who want a quiet space with internet. It provides a dedicated study environment without the limitations of public libraries.

Tips for Remote Workers in Kigali

Wherever you choose to work, here are a few practical tips:

  • Always have a mobile data backup. Rwandan networks like MTN and Airtel offer affordable data bundles that can serve as a fallback.
  • Choose a workspace close to where you stay to minimize commute time.
  • If you have regular video calls, prioritize spaces with stable internet and a quiet background.
  • Consider a monthly subscription if you work remotely most days. It saves money and guarantees you a consistent spot.
